Do Good by Doing Better - Brand Brief
Updated October 19, 2024

Overview:
Do Good by Doing Better is primarily published on Substack in the form of regular columns, lengthy in-depth pieces, and podcast episodes. These formats allow for a rich exploration of ideas and provide multiple ways for the audience to engage with the content.

Do Good by Doing Better is a reflective publication focused on exploring the intersections of technology, business, culture, and human behavior. Each piece is written by Eric Duell, the host and producer of Do Good by Doing Better. Through the lens of personal experiences and broader societal shifts, he aims to provide thoughtful insights into the ways we can align our actions, both small and large, with the goal of creating meaningful, positive change. The reflections are designed to encourage readers to be intentional, adaptable, and proactive, particularly when engaging with emerging technologies like Generative Al.

Core Purpose: The core purpose of Do Good by Doing Better is to inspire curiosity, encourage reflection, and promote a thoughtful approach to change. It serves as a guide for readers navigating technological advances, cultural shifts, and personal growth, ultimately encouraging individuals to leverage new opportunities to contribute positively to their lives and communities.

The publication was born out of a belief in the power of each individual to make a difference, even in small ways. The idea is to advance knowledge and understanding bit by bit, recognizing that while breakthrough moments are rare, the cumulative effect of learning, experimenting, and staying curious can lead to significant impact. The goal is to help readers "Up Your Impact - One Act at a Time," encouraging them to embrace growth and continuous improvement.

Voice and Tone: The voice of Do Good by Doing Better is reflective, conversational, and introspective, often blending personal storytelling with broader philosophical ideas. It strikes a balance between casual and contemplative, aiming to connect with readers on both an intellectual and emotional level. The tone is warm, engaging, and encourages readers to think deeply without feeling overwhelmed. It uses analogies and metaphors to make complex concepts more relatable and accessible.

Target Audience: The target audience includes professionals, lifelong learners, and those interested in technology, culture, and personal growth. They are curious individuals who seek thoughtful perspectives on change, are open to reflecting on their own experiences, and are motivated by the idea of contributing positively to their communities.

Visual and Design Elements: The visuals used for Do Good by Doing Better are consumer-oriented, bold, and evoke positive emotional responses through simplicity and strong graphic elements. They often represent both positive and challenging experiences, capturing the feelings associated with each. Photographs and designs, other than the logo, do not contain text, allowing the visuals to complement the narrative without overwhelming the message.

Content Structure:
Each reflection is structured to guide the reader through a journey:
- Introduction: Set the stage with a personal anecdote or observation that leads into the broader theme of the reflection.
- Exploration of Theme: Expands on the central theme, using metaphors and analogies to connect the personal story to broader ideas. Often explores multiple sides of the theme, avoiding black and white comparisons, blanket statements, and two-dimensional thinking in favor of a more multii-dimensional appreciation of the theme. The point is not to equivocate or give every point of view equal weight, but to show that we live in a world where multiple things can be true.
- Personal Insight or Reflection: Adds a personal dimension to the theme by reflecting on how it connects to Eric's and others' experiences. This section brings an introspective angle to the reflection, offering relatable takeaways, and framing the topic as understandable at a human scale.
- Deeper Analytical Dive: Provides a more detailed, analytical breakdown of a specific topic, tool, or concept. This section offers technical insights and critiques, helping the reader gain a more in-depth understanding of the topic at hand, and to help the reader become more of a practitioner if they choose to do so.
- Call to Action (How to Do Good by Doing Better and to Up Impact, One Act at a Time): Offers actionable steps that readers can take based on the reflection's theme and analysis. The aim is to inspire them to engage meaningfully with the topic.
- Conclusion: Summarizes the reflection and reinforces the main takeaway, tying back to the introduction and leaving readers with a forward-looking thought and encouragement.

Photo Bonus: At the end of each weekly Friday Morning Reflection, a 'Photo Bonus' is usually included, featuring a photograph taken by Eric. These photos often serve as part of the inspiration for the topic and provide a visual tie-in that makes the reflections more personal and helps readers connect on a deeper level.

Brand Personality: The brand personality is thoughtful, curious, inspired, and grounded. It values authenticity, depth, wisdom, and a balanced view of progress-recognizing that with every opportunity comes a responsibility to make informed and ethical choices. It seeks to be a companion on the reader's journey, offering insights that provoke reflection and inspire action.

Do Good by Doing Better is also deeply personal, stemming from a journey of realizing that one's voice can indeed be additive, even if not groundbreaking. The reflections aim to build the muscles of curiosity, inquiry, experimentation, and learning, with the belief that these incremental efforts create value and propel positive change.
